SECTION 144 BNS (BHARATIYA NYAYA SANHITA) - WRONGFUL RESTRAINT
Whoever voluntarily obstructs any person so as to prevent that person from proceeding in any direction in which that person has a right to proceed, is said wrongfully to restrain that person. Punishment: Simple imprisonment up to 1 month, or fine up to 5000 rupees, or both.

SECTION 103 BNS - EVICTION WITHOUT NOTICE
No tenant shall be evicted without serving a minimum notice period as prescribed under the state rent control act. Landlords must provide written notice 15 days in advance for monthly tenancies. Failure to comply makes the eviction illegal and the landlord liable under this section.

SECTION 115 BNS - GRIEVOUS HURT
Whoever causes grievous hurt to another person shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.

SECTION 74 BNS - ASSAULT OR CRIMINAL FORCE TO WOMAN WITH INTENT TO OUTRAGE HER MODESTY
Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years, and shall also be liable to fine.

SECTION 316 BNS - CRIMINAL BREACH OF TRUST
Whoever, being in any manner entrusted with property, or with any dominion over property, dishonestly misappropriates or converts to his own use that property, or dishonestly uses or disposes of that property in violation of any direction of law shall be punished with imprisonment up to 7 years.
