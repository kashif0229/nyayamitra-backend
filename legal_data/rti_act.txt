RTI ACT 2005 - SECTION 6: REQUEST FOR OBTAINING INFORMATION
A person who desires to obtain any information under the Act shall make a request in writing or through electronic means in English or Hindi or in the official language of the area to the Public Information Officer specifying the particulars of the information sought.

RTI ACT 2005 - SECTION 7: DISPOSAL OF REQUEST
The Central Public Information Officer or State Public Information Officer shall, as expeditiously as possible, and in any case within thirty days of the receipt of the request, either provide the information requested or reject the request for any of the reasons specified in sections 8 and 9.

RTI ACT 2005 - SECTION 8: EXEMPTION FROM DISCLOSURE
Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, there shall be no obligation to give any citizen information which would prejudicially affect the sovereignty and integrity of India, or which has been expressly forbidden to be published by any court of law.

RTI ACT - HOW TO FILE: The applicant must write an application to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the concerned department, pay a fee of Rs. 10 by cash/DD/postal order, and clearly state what information is required. The PIO must respond within 30 days.

RTI ACT 2005 - SECTION 19: APPEAL
If not satisfied with the response, the applicant can file a First Appeal within 30 days to the First Appellate Authority. A Second Appeal can be filed to the Central/State Information Commission within 90 days.
