PAYMENT OF WAGES ACT 1936
Every employer shall be responsible for the payment of wages to all employed persons. Wages must be paid within 7 working days after the end of the wage period. Failure to pay wages on time attracts a fine of Rs. 1500 to Rs. 7500 and compensation to the worker.

MINIMUM WAGES ACT 1948
No employer shall employ any worker in a scheduled employment at wages lower than the minimum wages fixed by the government for that class of work. Violation of minimum wages is a criminal offence punishable by up to 6 months imprisonment or fine up to Rs. 500 or both.

INDUSTRIAL DISPUTES ACT 1947 - WRONGFUL TERMINATION
No workman employed in any industry who has been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer shall be retrenched unless the employer gives 3 months notice or pays wages in lieu of notice and obtains prior government approval.

EMPLOYEES PROVIDENT FUND ACT 1952
Every employer is required to enroll employees earning up to Rs. 15,000 per month in the EPF scheme. The employer contributes 12% of the employee's basic salary to EPF. Failure to deduct or deposit EPF is a criminal offence. Employees can check their PF balance at epfindia.gov.in.

GRATUITY ACT 1972
Any employee who has completed 5 years of continuous service is entitled to gratuity. Gratuity = Last drawn salary × 15/26 × Number of years of service. The employer must pay gratuity within 30 days of the employee leaving service.
