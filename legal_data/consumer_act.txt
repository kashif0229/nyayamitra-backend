CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 - SECTION 2: DEFINITIONS
A consumer is any person who buys goods or avails services for consideration. This includes online purchases and services. Deficiency means any fault, imperfection, shortcoming in the quality, quantity, potency, purity or standard of services.

CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 - SECTION 35: COMPLAINT BEFORE DISTRICT COMMISSION
A complaint may be filed by a consumer, a registered consumer association, or the Central or State Government. District Consumer Commission handles cases up to Rs. 1 Crore. State Commission handles Rs. 1 Crore to Rs. 10 Crore. National Commission handles above Rs. 10 Crore.

CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 - SECTION 36: ADMISSION OF COMPLAINT  
The District Commission shall, within 21 days of receipt of the complaint, if it relates to goods, refer a copy to the opposite party. The opposite party shall be required to give their version of the case within 30 days.

CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 - DEFECTIVE PRODUCT RIGHTS
If a product is defective, the consumer has the right to replacement, repair, or full refund. The seller/manufacturer is liable for damages caused by the defective product. Consumer can also claim compensation for mental agony and legal costs.

CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT 2019 - SECTION 17: ESTABLISHMENT OF CONSUMER HELPLINE
The Central Government shall establish a national helpline. State Governments shall establish state-level consumer helplines. National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000 (Toll Free).
